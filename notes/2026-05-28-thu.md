### Story 1: Google DeepMind Solves 9 Unsolved Math Problems

Google DeepMind's AlphaProof Nexus solved nine open Erdős problems — some unsolved for 56 years — at a cost of just a few hundred dollars per problem. The system pairs an LLM with Lean, a proof assistant, to generate machine-verified proofs. It also proved 44 open conjectures from the Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences. This came just a day after OpenAI claimed its own Erdős breakthrough (disproving an 80-year-old conjecture).

- **Source:** The Rundown AI — "Google tops OpenAI's math breakthrough — 9 to 1" (May 25, 2026)

### Story 4: Anthropic & OpenAI Have Found Product-Market Fit

Simon Willison argues that both companies have found genuine product-market fit — not with consumer chat, but with coding agents. The evidence: both switched enterprise pricing from flat seats to API token pricing in April 2026. Willison himself gets $2,180/month in API value for $200 in subscription costs. Enterprise customers are now paying full API rates, and both companies are planning IPOs. The key insight: coding agents burn vastly more tokens than chat, and companies will pay $200+/month/user for real productivity gains.
